How ChatGPT can help you write faster, and ways to use it

Read time: 5min

February 27, 2023


I got the idea to write about ChatGPT when my friend told me that it is plagiarism to use it for writing content. So I thought that people might think that it actually is, so let us first look at that.



What is ChatGPT?


ChatGPT is a Natural Pretrained Transformer that is built on AI technology. The most compelling thing about it is that it gives human-like responses.


Is it plagiarism if I use ChatGPT to write something?


I would argue yes and no. If you give a prompt that will write the whole text out for you, then technically, it is.


If you use it strategically in your text, for example you get a writer block and ask ChatGPT for ideas to write about, then I would say it is fine, you are still learning and providing value. Remember to be clear in your writing, put the actions as you would execute yourself and see the magic. If you are a programmer, think of it in terms of coding, you have to write specific code for the system to understand what you want. The thing with ChatGPT is that you do not need to know how to code, but be precise in your speech.

Can you write whole blogs with ChatGPT?


In short, yes. I would not recommend doing so though. Believe me, when it came out, the first thing in my mind was “I won't have to do any research! I can just write what I want to write and it is complete!”, I was wrong, don't be fooled. It will lead to something like this:


    1. You write a prompt

    2. Get the text

    3. Change some words so you can say you wrote it

    4. Learn almost nothing about the topic

    5. Damage yourself and the reader

    6. Do it again because it is easy



Conclusion


Use it to get ideas, not final products. Ideas can produce multiple outcomes depending on the person performing the activity.


If you are looking to get a finished product from ChatGPT and never doing your own work, please don't use it. My previous blog was with ChatGPT. I only did it to prove it can be done. I encourage you to look at it after this.


Believe me or not, I probably spent less time writing this one than the one with ChatGPT, because of the time I spent checking for any wrong outputs, prompting to write sentences in different ways and prolonging the sentences to meet the word count.


Trust me it is not worth the hassle for the quality of content it produces. You can do much better with some practice.


Also, don't forget to thank ChatGPT for the assistance, so it doesn't start hating you and stops replying without prior notice because you forgot about its feelings. (This is satire, but I still do it)
